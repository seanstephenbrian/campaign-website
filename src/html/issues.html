Key Issues

Let's put students first.

As a member of the District 65 Board of Education, John Martin will work diligently to put the needs of students first.

Click to learn more

John's core ideas include:

  • Returning rigorous academics as a top priority for D65
  • A belief that lowering college preparedness standards is not equity or a strategy for success
  • Aligning D65 curriculum with D202 so that students are fully prepared for high school and beyond; the current system provides false encouragement to students
  • The millions of dollars spent on consultants and administrators take funds away from academics and teachers' focus away from teaching
  • Equity and excellence are not mutually exclusive
  • Diverting focus from academics has resulted in inequity among disadvantaged students
  • D65 should restore advanced math path for its students, including geometry
    • It’s tragic that it’s gone — it helped countless D65 students over many years
    • Not providing it promotes inequity and is harmful most to disadvantaged students, setting them further behind when moving on to university

Let’s rebuild trust and re-commit to public education.

District 65 must foster a better relationship with the teachers and parents. School leaders should treat everyone with respect and must work collaboratively with others, even when opinions differ.

John recognizes the issues that have arisen due to D65's failure to prioritize transparency and accountability:

Transparency in finances:

  • D65 has more than $300m in revenue and is not managed as such

Transparency in decision-making:

  • Shortcomings in transparency and accountability at D65 have resulted in:
    • Lowering of academic standards

      • Similar to inflated CEO salaries, this inequality is harmful to employees, staff, teachers, parents, and students
