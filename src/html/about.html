About John Martin

Motivation

John has a deep-rooted belief in public education, and therefore reinstating academics as the top priority for all D65 students, faculty, staff, and parents. The child of a teacher, John holds the utmost respect for teachers and students, and looks to both work with school management and hold them accountable to the community.

Leader

When John sees a need he steps in. He likes to put ideas into action—taking conceptual ideas and transforming them into concrete steps. Being results driven provides a way to ensure benefits reach everyone equally. Motivated by more than just the results, but how they impact the people involved.

Experience

John’s love for youth sports motivated him to become the Commissioner of AYSO Evanston during the COVID pandemic. Through his leadership, soccer returned to the fields of Evanston after a one-year absence, and efficiencies were gained which led to a reduction in participation fees paid by families. And most importantly, the kids had fun.

Background

John moved to Evanston in 2015 with his wife and two children. His children both attend elementary school in D65, currently in first and second grades. John grew up in Cincinnati and moved to Chicago shortly after completing his Bachelor of Science in Business Administration at Miami University in Oxford, Ohio. While working at an accounting firm, John completed his Master of Science in Taxation at DePaul University. John now works for a software company based in Chicago.
